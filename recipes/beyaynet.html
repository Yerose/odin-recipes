BEYAYNET

Yetsom Beyaynetu (Ethiopian Combination Platter)

Beyaynet, which is a popular Ethiopian vegetarian dish.

typically served as a platter of various vegetable and legume dishes, which are traditionally eaten with injera,

Ingredients

  • Injera: a sourdough flatbread made from teff flour, which is used to scoop up the stews and vegetables on the platter.
  • Lentils: 1 cup of red lentils used to make spicy lentil stews like misir wot.
  • Peas: 1 cup of split peas
  • Onion: 1 onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Ginger: 2 tablespoons of ginger, minced
  • Berbere: 2 tablespoons of berbere spice blend
  • Niter Kibbeh: 2 tablespoons of niter kibbeh (Ethiopian clarified butter)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Rinse the lentils and split peas in a colander under running water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a large pot, add the lentils and split peas and cover them with water. Bring the water to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the lentils and split peas simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until they are fully cooked and tender.
  4. In a separate pan, melt the niter kibbeh over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and minced ginger to the pan and saut√© them until the onions are translucent.
  5. Add the berbere spice blend to the pan and stir well until it's evenly mixed with the onion, garlic, and ginger mixture.
  6. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger mixture to the pot with the lentils and split peas. Stir well.
  7. Add salt to taste and stir again.
  8. Continue to cook the beyaynet for an additional 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches your desired consistency.
  9. Serve the beyaynet hot with injera (Ethiopian flatbread) or rice.
