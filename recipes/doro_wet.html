DOROWET

Doro wot - Ethiopian chicken stew slow simmered with berbere (Chili spice blend)

Doro Wot is a traditional Ethiopian dish that is often considered the national dish of Ethiopia.

It is a spicy chicken stew that is usually served with injera, a sourdough flatbread that is a staple of Ethiopian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (traditionally free-range)
  • Berbere: 2-3 tablespoons berbere spice blend
  • Onion: 2 large onions, chopped
  • Garlic: 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • Niter Kibbeh: 4 tablespoons niter kibbeh (clarified butter infused with spices)
  • Tomato2-3 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • Chicken broth: 1 cup chicken broth or water
  • Egg: 4 hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Cilantro: Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the niter kibbeh in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and cook until they are soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and ginger and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the berbere spice blend to the pot and stir well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and stir well. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Pour in the chicken broth or water and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  7. Cover the pot and let the chicken cook for 30-40 minutes, or until it is cooked through and tender.
  8. If using, add the hard-boiled eggs to the pot during the last 10-15 minutes of cooking.
  9. Add salt to taste.
  10. Serve the Doro Wot hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or parsley, with injera or rice on the side.
